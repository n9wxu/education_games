# Game request: Skate 'n' Add

- **Requested by:** Veronica
- **Subject:** math   **Goal:** stretch a favorite subject
- **Target age/grade:** around 6-7 (1st grade)

## Summary
You roller skate around and grab the floating star that has the right answer to an adding problem — with a fun QUACK when you get it right!

## Skills practiced / tested
- Single-digit addition
- Recognizing the correct sum among choices
- Quick mental math under a chosen speed

## Gameplay
A simple addition problem appears (like 2 + 3). Several numbered stars float by. The player moves their roller-skating character to skate into and grab the star showing the correct sum. A speed dial lets the player choose how fast the stars move.

## Multiplayer model (must not let players disrupt each other's learning)
Observe-only with an optional cooperative team score. Players see other kids' skating characters nearby doing their own math, but each child's stars and problems are their own — no one can grab or block another player's stars. An optional team button lets players opt into a shared score for correct stars grabbed; it never forces wrong answers or interferes with anyone's play.

## Look & feel
Colorful, friendly cartoon roller-skating characters gliding on a rink, glowing numbered stars floating by, cheerful and simple. Sound: a silly duck "QUACK" when the correct star is grabbed.

## Acceptance criteria (testable)
- Given an addition problem is shown, When three or more numbered stars float by, Then exactly one star shows the correct sum.
- Given the player skates into the correct star, When it is grabbed, Then a QUACK sound plays and the player earns a point.
- Given the player grabs an incorrect star, When it is grabbed, Then no point is awarded and the player can try again without penalty forcing a wrong answer.
- Given the speed dial is adjusted, When set slower or faster, Then the stars' float speed changes accordingly.
- Given other players are online, When the player looks around, Then they see other skaters but cannot grab or block another player's stars.
- Given the team button is turned on, When teammates grab correct stars, Then a shared team score increases; When turned off, Then each player keeps their own score.
- Given a correct answer is given repeatedly, Then problems remain single-digit addition appropriate for 1st grade.
