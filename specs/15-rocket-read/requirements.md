# Game request: Rocket Read

- **Requested by:** yuo
- **Subject:** reading   **Goal:** shore up a hard skill
- **Target age/grade:** 11 years old

## Summary
You read a short sentence, type the answer to a quick question about it, and your rocket blasts up through space!

## Skills practiced / tested
- Reading comprehension of short sentences
- Understanding who/what/where in a sentence
- Typing a correct short answer

## Gameplay
A short sentence appears over a real space photo, followed by a simple comprehension question (e.g., who, what, or where). The player types the answer. A correct answer plays a 'ding' and whooshes the player's rocket upward; the pace is brisk/fast to keep it exciting.

## Multiplayer model (must not let players disrupt each other's learning)
Observe-only/parallel play: each child flies their own rocket and reads their own sentences at their own pace. Players can see friends' rockets zooming alongside, creating a friendly race feel, but no one can change, block, or force answers on another player, so no child's learning can be disrupted.

## Look & feel
Real-looking space photos (public-domain/NASA-style imagery) with stars and planets. Sounds: a happy 'ding' for correct answers and a 'whoosh' as the rocket flies up.

## Acceptance criteria (testable)
- Given a short sentence and a comprehension question, when the player types the correct answer, then a 'ding' plays and the rocket moves up with a 'whoosh'.
- Given the player types an incorrect answer, then the rocket does not advance and the player may try again, with no penalty forced by other players.
- Given multiple players in the same session, when one player answers, then only their own rocket is affected and other players' sentences/answers are unchanged.
- Given the game is running, when sentences appear, then they display over public-domain space imagery.
- Given a correct answer streak, then the pace/appearance of sentences stays brisk to match a fast play speed.
- Given a player views the screen, then friends' rockets are visible but cannot be blocked or altered by that player.
